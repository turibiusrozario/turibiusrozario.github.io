Impact Networks is a book on creating networks for positive social changes. This is different from hierarchical structures such as most companies and project teams. I was recommended this book and overall appreciated the insights. The challenge is that many of the things require _practice_. This is something we need to do on our own. I would still recommend this book for anyone trying to create community for positive actions.

I have included the page number where the notes are from; this is for the physical book.

- Page 190, don't do consensus based decision making; do consent-based. Have decisions fall within people's range of tolerance
- Page 191, steps for consent-based decision making
  1.  Context: Consider the decisions already made, the circumstances that decision has to be made in
  2.  Clarification: Allow participants to ask questions but not evaluate. Encourage others to consider purpose, principles, and benefits to the network/themselves
  3.  Consent: Restate purpose. Then ask for vote. Vote with 0 to 5:
      - 5: lead
      - 4: partner
      - 3: follow
      - 2: concern - will be tracking. Anything below this is dissent
      - 1: caution - major concerns, need discussion
      - 0: oppose - needs changes, outside of tolerance
  4.  Have 0s and 1s meet with 4s and 5s to resolve issues
- Page 195, fundamental evaluation criteria for networks

    - [ ] Network Participant Survey. [Link](https://www.converge.net/toolkit/network-participant-survey)
    - [ ] Indicators of Network Health. [Link](https://www.converge.net/toolkit/indicators-of-network-health)
  - Emergent activity: how much coordinate work/collaboration are being sparked by the participants themselves
